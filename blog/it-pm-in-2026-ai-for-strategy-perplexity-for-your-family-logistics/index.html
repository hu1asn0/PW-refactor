IT PM in 2026: AI for Strategy, Perplexity for Your Family Logistics

András Simon

As an IT project manager, you’re entering Q1 2026 planning season while also juggling year-end travel, family logistics, and holiday timing puzzles. Perplexity﻿ can be the same assistant for both: helping you scan 2026 trends, validate budgets, prioritise your portfolio – and at the same time plan a sane, realistic family trip.

1. Professional area – Q1 2026 planning with Perplexity

1.1. Step-by-step example – Trend research and Q1 focus

Scenario:
You need a Q1 roadmap that reflects 2026 realities, not last year’s assumptions. You want clarity on emerging technologies, methods, and best practices.

Step 1 – Build your trend radar

Use a high-level prompt:

„Summarise the key technology and project management trends expected in 2026 for the IT sector. Focus on:﻿ - agile/lean & product-oriented ways of working;﻿ - DevOps/Platform Engineering directions;﻿ - AI-powered tools (code, testing, documentation, planning).﻿ Provide 5–7 concrete trends with short explanations and typical use cases.”﻿

This becomes your “trend radar” slide.

Step 2 – Select what matters for Q1

Then:

„From the above list, select the 5 most relevant trends for a mid/large IT organisation working with enterprise clients in Q1 2026. For each, describe:﻿ - the expected business impact;﻿ - implementation complexity;﻿ - a suggested first pilot step.”﻿

Now you have a Q1-relevant shortlist to discuss with leadership.

Step 3 – Draft Q1 workshop agenda

„Create a 60–90 minute agenda for a Q1 2026 trends & roadmap workshop with leadership, where we translate these trends into concrete backlog items.”﻿

You get a ready-made agenda with timeboxes and discussion topics.

1.2. Cookbook – Budget validation & portfolio prioritisation

Recipe name: “Q1 Budget & Portfolio Prioritisation – One AI-assisted session instead of three meetings”﻿

Ingredients:

  • List of planned Q1 projects (estimated costs, expected business value, risk)

  • Known vendor/service cost ranges where available

  • 60–90 focused minutes before the budget is frozen

Preparation:

  1. Budget sanity check

    „Here are our typical service cost assumptions (e.g. daily rates, cloud hosting, licences). Compare them with current market ranges and flag where we’re too optimistic or too conservative.”﻿

  2. Generate a scoring matrix

    „For the following projects, score (1–5) expected business value, strategic fit, implementation complexity, and risk level. Suggest an overall priority score and sort the list.”﻿

  3. Quick ROI estimates

    „Provide rough ROI estimates over 12–24 months for these projects using typical industry benchmarks.”﻿

  4. Leadership summary

    „Draft a 1-page ‘Q1 2026 Portfolio & Budget Overview’ for senior leadership: top 5 projects with rationale, budget risks, and where deferring or re-prioritising might help.”﻿

Result: a defensible portfolio and budget story in one AI-supported working block.

2. Personal area – Travel planning & logistics with Perplexity

Photo by Annie Spratt on Unsplash

2.1. Step-by-step example – Family Christmas trip

Scenario:
You’re planning a Christmas or year-end family trip. You need child-friendly destinations, reasonable travel time, and a plan that respects everyone’s energy levels.

Step 1 – Shortlist destinations

„Suggest 5–7 family-friendly destinations in Europe around Christmas that:﻿ - are reachable within 2–3 hours by plane or train;﻿ - are safe;﻿ - offer family/child-friendly activities and Christmas atmosphere;﻿ - have decent last-minute deal potential.”﻿

Step 2 – Filter for last-minute friendliness

"From the above, choose the 3 destinations with the best last-minute deal potential and summarize what to watch out for in terms of value for money.”﻿

Step 3 – Route optimization

"Recommend the most reasonable way to travel (car vs. train vs. plane), given holiday traffic, delay risks, and connection complexity.”﻿

Step 4 – Programs & stays

"Propose a 3-day ‘light’ itinerary for a family trip: Christmas markets, 1–2 cultural activities, and one slower day for rest.”﻿ „Describe 3–5 types of accommodation that fit a family, with decision criteria (location, noise, kitchen, space).”﻿

2.2. Cookbook – Minimal-stress holiday travel planning

Recipe name: “Holiday Travel Planner – PM edition with Perplexity”﻿

Ingredients:

  • Origin, destination(s), dates

  • Number of travelers, ages, special needs

  • Flexibility window (fixed vs. +/- 1–2 days)

Preparation:

  1. Compare travel options

    „Compare car, train, and plane options across time, cost, comfort, stress level, and holiday traffic risk for this route and dates.”﻿

  2. Create a travel risk register

    „List 7–10 typical travel risks around the holidays (delays, weather, traffic, overbookings) with mitigation ideas for each.”﻿

  3. Build buffers

    „Suggest reasonable buffer times for departure and return, considering peak travel times.”﻿

  4. One-page travel plan

„Draft a 1-page ‘Travel Plan’ I can send to my family, summarising departure time, key stops, breaks, backup plans, and who is responsible for what.”﻿
