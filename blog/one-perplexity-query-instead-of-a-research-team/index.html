One Perplexity Query Instead of a Research Team

András Simon

Introduction

Imagine this: You need to evaluate a new development stack, but you don’t know where to start. The traditional approach? Open multiple tabs, spend 2-3 hours browsing, then hold meetings where nobody feels confident the decisions are data-backed.

With Perplexity, we do it differently. This AI-powered research tool isn’t just for answering questions – it’s actually one of the most efficient allies IT project managers can have in their workflows.

Over the past year, numerous IT teams have discovered that Perplexity isn’t just a search engine, but an intelligent research partner that provides time savings, supports decisions with verified data, and enables faster decision-making.

Why This Matters Now

The typical IT project manager’s day looks like this:

  • 9:00 AM – Planning meeting where technical questions arise

  • 9:30 AM – Spending the last hour examining another tool

  • 2:00 PM – Need to propose a solution to the client, but research is incomplete

  • 4:00 PM – Another meeting to say “we need to investigate further”

Perplexity eliminates this cycle. It doesn’t replace thinking, but it accelerates information intake for decision-making.

📋 Practical Example: Step-by-Step

Case: Choosing Between Microservices vs Monolithic Architecture

You’re the IT project manager at a 50-person development team. Before a 3-month sprint, you need to decide on the new system’s architecture. The architecture team has already spent 2 days researching, but the information feels incomplete.

Step 1: Create a Structured Query

Open Perplexity (web.perplexity.com) and create a Research mode search. Don’t ask simple questions – structure your needs:

Query:
"Compare microservices and monolithic architectures for a 
50-person development team. Consider:
- Development speed in the first 6 months
- Operational complexity
- Scalability
- Suitability for team size
- Typical pitfalls that teams have experienced
Please answer with source-backed recommendations."

Step 2: Upload PDF/Documents

If you already have a half-finished analysis or prior decision documents:

  • Click the “Upload” button

  • Upload your previous research

  • Perplexity automatically integrates the information into new responses

Query:

"Based on our previous document, how would we develop a microservices 
strategy that would fit our current team capabilities?"

Step 3: Create Summaries

After you receive the result:

  • Ask Perplexity: "Create a 1-minute presentation extract from these points"

  • The AI automatically generates editable text

  • Copy and paste into PowerPoint or your document

Step 4: Generate Stakeholder Communication

One more query:

"Write a 2-paragraph email to leadership explaining why we should 
choose [selected architecture]. Reference the previous analysis 
and associated risks."

Result

  • Time Saved: 6 hours of research → 45 minutes of structured analysis

  • Decision Basis: Informed decisions backed by data rather than intuition

  • Stakeholder Communication: Ready-to-use communication template that builds confidence

🍳 Cookbook: Requirements Management & Risk Analysis

Recipe: Accelerating Requirements Analysis

Structure

1. INPUT: Document containing requirements (e.g., RFP or client brief)
2. PROCESS: Perplexity Research Mode
3. OUTPUT: Structured requirements list + risk analysis

Steps

1. Upload the Document

Enter Perplexity, select Research mode, and click the Upload button. Upload:

  • Client requirements list (Word doc, PDF)

  • Preliminary specification

  • Document containing business objectives

2. The Analysis Question

Query:
"Organize and categorize these requirements as follows:
- Must-have (Critical)
- Should-have (Important)
- Nice-to-have (Optional)

Identify any requirements that might conflict with each other, 
and suggest solutions for those conflicts. Also list implicit 
requirements that aren't mentioned but may need to be addressed."

3. Risk Assessment

Query:
"Based on these requirements, what are the potential risks in a 
software development project? Categorize them as technical, business, 
and organizational risks. For each risk, suggest a mitigation strategy."

4. Use the Outputs

  • Copy the structured lists into a shared document

  • Remember the archives – Perplexity saves files on the Assets tab

  • Share the requirements list with the client for confirmation

Real Case Study: IT Infrastructure Upgrade

Client Need: “We need to upgrade our data center, but we don’t know what options we have.”

Perplexity Analysis (in one query):

"Evaluate a corporate data center upgrade project. The client's needs are:
- 40% cost reduction
- 99.95% uptime
- On-premise and hybrid options
- 18-month implementation window

Recommend advantageous architectures (on-premise, hybrid, cloud), 
quantify ROI, and identify hidden costs."

Output (delivered in 10-15 minutes):

  • Three-way comparison (On-premise vs Hybrid vs Cloud)

  • ROI calculations with specific estimates

  • Scheduling risks and mitigation options

  • Competitive recommendations (e.g., vendor solutions to consider)

💡 Perplexity in Your IT PM Workflow – Daily Ideas

1. Daily Standup Preparation (5 minutes saved)

Query:
"Summarize the key points from Jira activity over the last 3 days [CSV file].
Create a 30-second standup summary."

2. Draft Client Emails

Query:
"Write a professional email to the client about the current delay on 
[project name]. Explain the reason, suggest the solution, and provide 
transparency regarding the future timeline."

3. Translate Technical Concepts for Non-Technical Audiences

Query:
"Explain [technical concept, e.g., APIs, microservices, blockchain] 
in a way that a non-technical executive can understand."

4. Project Retrospective Analysis

Query:
"Evaluate a completed project based on these metrics [data]. 
What contributed to its success, and what lessons can we apply 
to future projects?"

🤝 The Human Factor Still Matters

A decision made with Perplexity is only as good as the query you ask.

Best Practices:

  • Be specific in your questions

  • Provide context about your goals

  • Use Research mode for complex topics

  • Always critically evaluate the results

  • Check citations – Perplexity marks all sources
